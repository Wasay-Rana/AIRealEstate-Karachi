Karachi Real Estate: Legal Framework, Taxes, and Compliance (2024–2025)

Governing Authorities

Multiple regulatory bodies govern property transactions in Karachi:

1. Sindh Revenue Board (SRB): Collects stamp duty and capital value tax (CVT) on property transfers. Issues SRB challan receipts as proof of payment.

2. Sindh Board of Revenue (SBoR): Maintains land records for non-cantonment Karachi. Manages DCRS (District Computerized Revenue System). Issues Fard (ownership certificate) for non-DHA, non-cantonment properties.

3. Federal Board of Revenue (FBR): Sets property valuation tables used for tax computation. Issues FGRF (FBR Gross Revenue Form). FBR rates are revised periodically and differ significantly from market rates.

4. SBCA (Sindh Building Control Authority): Issues building plans, NOCs, and completion certificates. From 2024, operates a digital NOC verification system.

5. DHA Karachi: Self-regulatory authority for DHA Karachi and DHA City Karachi. Transfers within DHA are handled internally; SRB stamp duty still applies.

6. Bahria Town (private): Manages internal records for BTK. SRB stamp duty applies on BTK transfers.

7. Cantonment Boards: Cliff ton, Malir, Manora — jurisdiction over cantonment housing schemes.

Sindh Finance Act 2024 — Key Changes

The Sindh Finance Act 2024 introduced several property-related tax changes effective from July 1, 2024:

Stamp Duty Rates (Revised):
- Transactions up to PKR 5,000,000: 3% of transaction value
- Transactions PKR 5,000,001 to PKR 20,000,000: 4% of transaction value
- Transactions above PKR 20,000,000: 5% of transaction value

Pre-2024 rates were: 3% flat up to PKR 3 million, 4% above. The 2024 revision raised the upper threshold and added a 5% slab for high-value transactions, significantly increasing the tax burden on Bahria Town Karachi and DHA premium plot transfers.

Capital Value Tax (CVT):
- Residential plots and houses: 2% of FBR valuation (no change from 2023)
- Commercial properties: 4% of FBR valuation (up from 3% in 2022)
- Agricultural land converted to residential: 3% (new slab, 2024)
- Cantonment residential: 1% (lower rate reconfirmed in 2024 Finance Act clarification)

Withholding Tax (Federal — Income Tax Ordinance 2001, Section 236C/236K):
- Section 236C: 1% withholding on seller (non-filer: 2%)
- Section 236K: 1% advance tax on buyer (non-filer: 2%)
- These are federal taxes collected at the time of transfer registration

Important: Tax slab at time of transaction applies, not current rates. SRB challan receipts must record the applicable slab and effective date to enable accurate duty calculations for historical transfers.

FBR Property Valuation Table — July 2024 Revision

FBR revised its Karachi property valuation table effective July 2024, significantly increasing rates across all major schemes:

DHA City Karachi:
- Residential (any size): PKR 18,000–22,000 per sq yard (up from PKR 8,000–10,000 in 2022)
- Commercial: PKR 55,000 per sq yard

DHA Karachi (Phase 1–8):
- Phase 1–4: PKR 90,000–125,000 per sq yard (residential)
- Phase 5–6: PKR 70,000–95,000 per sq yard
- Phase 7–8: PKR 55,000–75,000 per sq yard

Bahria Town Karachi:
- Precincts 1–20: PKR 25,000–35,000 per sq yard
- Precincts 21–36: PKR 18,000–28,000 per sq yard
- Precincts 37–45 (Phase 2): PKR 12,000–16,000 per sq yard (new rate, 2024)

Malir City (MCDA):
- 120 sq yard: PKR 12,000 per sq yard
- 200 sq yard: PKR 14,000 per sq yard

Gulshan-e-Iqbal / North Nazimabad:
- Category: PKR 35,000–55,000 per sq yard depending on block

Note: FBR rates are substantially lower than market rates. The stamp duty is calculated on whichever is higher: declared sale price or FBR valuation. Under-declaration of sale price (to reduce stamp duty) is an offense under the SRB Act but was historically common.

SBCA Digital NOC System (2024)

SBCA launched a digital NOC portal in 2024 as part of Sindh's e-governance initiative. Key features:
- Verification URL format: https://sbca.gos.pk/noc-verify/[NOC-Number]
- NOC types: Building Permission, Completion Certificate, No Objection for Transfer
- Processing time: 15–30 working days (digital) vs 60–90 days (manual prior to 2024)
- NOC validity: 2 years from issuance
- Digital NOC includes QR code for field verification

For property transactions in non-cantonment schemes, SRB now requires SBCA digital NOC reference at the time of stamp duty payment for transactions above PKR 10 million. This is new as of 2024.

Litigation and Courts

Property disputes in Karachi are handled by:
- Civil courts (for general property disputes)
- Sindh High Court (SHC) for constitutional challenges and high-value matters
- Revenue courts (for land record / mutation disputes)
- Cantonment Tribunal (for cantonment scheme disputes)
- DHA Internal Dispute Committee (first instance) → SHC if unresolved

Cases involving Bahria Town Karachi ownership are handled at SHC level given the Supreme Court's original jurisdiction history.

Common causes of litigation in major Karachi schemes:
1. Fraudulent transfers (seller transfers without disclosing existing mortgage/encumbrance)
2. Multiple registrations on same plot (data entry or collusion errors)
3. Legal heir disputes (allottee dies; multiple heirs claim)
4. Encroachment by adjacent plot holder
5. Non-delivery of possession (buyer sues developer for delay)

Naya Pakistan Housing Authority (NPHA) Karachi

The Naya Pakistan Housing Programme (now under National Housing Authority post-2022 government change) had allocated plots in Karachi under the Naya Pakistan Low-Cost Housing Scheme.

Key Karachi NPHA allocations:
- Surjani Town scheme: 1,000 sq ft apartments; first ballots 2021; construction ongoing 2024
- Korangi scheme: smaller plot allocation; 80 sq yard plots
- Federal government scheme: separate from Sindh provincial schemes

NPHA data is available in PDF format from the Ministry of Housing website and includes allotment letters with applicant CNIC, plot number, scheme code, and payment plan.

Important distinction: NPHA schemes are on federal land (not subject to SRB stamp duty in the same way; separate federal stamp tax applies). Buyers should not confuse NPHA allotments with DHA or Bahria files.

Property Market Regulatory Developments 2025

1. Anti-Money Laundering: SRB began cross-referencing high-value property transactions with FBR returns in 2024 as part of AML compliance. Transactions above PKR 50 million are flagged for additional scrutiny.

2. Foreigner Property Rights: The Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan (SECP) issued updated guidelines in 2024 for overseas Pakistani property purchases; digital ROIP (Registration of Immovable Property) certificates now available.

3. Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs): Two new REITs launched in Karachi in 2024, providing a regulated investment vehicle for Karachi commercial real estate exposure without direct property purchase.
