Karachi Property Price Index — Quarterly Data 2023–2025

Data Sources and Methodology

Price data compiled from multiple sources:
- Zameen.com quarterly price index (published Q1 2024, Q2 2024, Q3 2024, Q4 2024, Q1 2025)
- Graana.com listing price averages (scraped/exported quarterly)
- Agent surveys conducted by major brokerages (PBCA member firms)
- Direct transaction data where available from SRB challans

Methodology: Price per sq yard calculated from actual listing prices and completed transactions. Outliers (distressed sales, top-of-market prestige transactions) excluded. Index benchmarked to Q1 2023 = 100.

Note on Graana vs Zameen: Graana.com gained significant market share in Karachi by 2024–2025 and tends to list slightly higher asking prices than Zameen due to its premium agent network. Zameen.com index reflects a broader transaction base.

Market Commentary

Overall Karachi property market 2024–2025 trends:

1. Dollar stabilization effect: After PKR/USD rate stabilized in H2 2023 at around PKR 280-290/USD, speculative dollar-hedging buying slowed. This caused a correction in mid-tier BTK precincts and some DCK sectors.

2. Possession premium: Properties with confirmed possession (DCK Sector 1–8, BTK Precinct 1–20) trade at 25–40% premium over under-development or file-only properties.

3. Overseas Pakistani demand: Despite PKR stabilization, demand from UK, UAE, and Saudi-based Pakistanis remained strong, particularly for DCK (affordable in dollar terms) and BTK Golf City.

4. Rental yield improvement: As BTK and DCK communities become more populated, rental yields have improved from sub-1% (2021) to 2–3% (2025) making buy-to-rent more viable.

5. Commercial plot premium: Commercial plots in DCK and BTK command 3–5x per-sq-yard premium over residential, reflecting limited commercial supply relative to residential demand.
