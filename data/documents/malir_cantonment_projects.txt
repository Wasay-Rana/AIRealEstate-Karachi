Malir Cantonment Housing Projects — Malir City and Malir Town Residencia (2024–2025)

Overview of Malir Cantonment Development Authority

The Malir Cantonment Development Authority (MCDA) manages housing projects under Malir Cantonment jurisdiction. Unlike civilian housing schemes, cantonment-based projects fall under the Cantonment Act and are regulated by the respective Cantonment Board rather than SBCA or KMC. This provides a degree of legal certainty that some buyers find reassuring.

Malir City

Malir City is MCDA's flagship residential project. It is located in Malir Cantonment area, approximately 20–25 km from Saddar, Karachi, off the Korangi-Landhi corridor.

Plot Categories and Sizes:
- Category A: 80 sq yard (smaller residential units)
- Category B: 120 sq yard (most popular category)
- Category C: 200 sq yard
- Category D: 400 sq yard (premium residential)
- Commercial plots: 60–100 sq yard strips within each residential block

Ballot History:
- Phase 1 Ballot: 2016 — residential plots in Blocks A through G
- Phase 2 Ballot: 2019 — Blocks H through N
- Phase 3 Ballot (2024): Major allocation round in Q2 2024 covering approximately 3,200 new plots in Blocks O through U. This was the largest single ballot in MCDA history.

Phase 3 2024 Ballot Details:
MCDA conducted the ballot on May 15, 2024. Allotment letters were dispatched via registered mail and courier, with digital records uploaded to the MCDA online portal. Applicants with CNIC-verified accounts received SMS confirmation within 72 hours.

Files from Phase 3 2024 ballot carry the prefix MC-2024-P3-[Block]-[Plot Number] in MCDA's records. These are fresh allotments and have not been transferred yet as of Q1 2025 (cooling period of 6 months applies before transfer is permitted).

Plot Prices (Malir City):

120 sq yard residential:
- Phase 1 blocks (A–G): PKR 38–52 lakh (resale market, 2025)
- Phase 2 blocks (H–N): PKR 32–45 lakh (2025)
- Phase 3 blocks (O–U, fresh 2024 allotment): PKR 28–38 lakh (primary market + small resale premium)

200 sq yard residential:
- Phase 1: PKR 65–85 lakh
- Phase 2: PKR 55–72 lakh
- Phase 3: PKR 48–65 lakh

Development Status:
- Blocks A–N (Phases 1 and 2): Developed; roads, electricity, water, sewage in place. Occupied.
- Blocks O–T (Phase 3): Under development. Roads graded; utilities not yet commissioned.
- Block U: Demarcation only; construction not yet begun.

Allotment Document Format (for parsing):
MCDA allotment letters are issued as A4 PDF documents with the following fields:
- Allotment Letter Number (format: MCDA/MC/2024/XXXXX)
- Applicant Name and CNIC
- Plot Number and Block
- Category (80/120/200/400 sq yard)
- Date of Allotment
- Total Amount Payable (lump sum or installment schedule)
- Development Charges per sq yard
- Terms and Conditions (standard 12 clauses)

Excel-based allotment sheets (bulk format) contain: Serial Number, CNIC, Applicant Name, Plot No., Block, Category, Amount Paid, Balance Due, Allotment Status. These are used by MCDA for internal tracking and provided to applicants on request.

Malir Town Residencia

Malir Town Residencia (MTR) is a companion project under MCDA, positioned slightly closer to the Karachi city boundary. It targets middle-income buyers who cannot afford DHA or Bahria prices but want cantonment-level security of tenure.

Plot categories: 80, 100, 150, and 240 sq yard residential; 40–60 sq yard commercial.

Ballot Rounds:
- 2017: Phase 1 ballot — Blocks A through F (approximately 1,800 plots)
- 2021: Phase 2 ballot — Blocks G through L (approximately 2,200 plots)
- 2024: Phase 3 ballot announced; applications closed October 2024; ballot expected Q2 2025

Malir Town Residencia 2024 Application Details:
Application fee: PKR 5,000 per category applied for. Applicants could apply in up to 2 categories. Priority was given to serving and retired cantonment employees. Maximum 2 allotments per CNIC.

Transfer Rules (MTR):
- No transfer allowed within first 2 years of allotment (anti-speculative lock-in)
- After 2 years: transfer fee = PKR 200 per sq yard for residential, PKR 500 per sq yard for commercial
- CNIC of transferee must not already hold an allotment in same scheme

Stamp Duty and CVT for Cantonment Projects

Cantonment-based housing schemes (Malir City, Malir Town Residencia, Clifton Cantonment projects) have a different stamp duty treatment:

For intra-cantonment transfers:
- Stamp duty payable at SRB: 3% on declared value (FBR rate or market rate, higher)
- CVT: 1% (cantonment projects get a lower CVT rate compared to civilian schemes per Sindh Finance Act 2024 clarification)
- No KMC or CDGK levies apply (cantonment jurisdiction)

FBR Valuation Rates (Malir Cantonment Area, July 2024 Revision):
- 120 sq yard plots: FBR rate PKR 12,000 per sq yard
- 200 sq yard plots: FBR rate PKR 14,000 per sq yard
- 400 sq yard plots: FBR rate PKR 16,000 per sq yard
(These rates are significantly below market rates but are used for tax calculation purposes)

Key Advantages of Cantonment Projects

1. Legal clarity: Cantonment Board has exclusive jurisdiction; less prone to political interference
2. Security of tenure: Less risk of land encroachment compared to civilian schemes
3. Maintained infrastructure: Cantonment Boards historically maintain better roads, green belts, and utilities
4. Exclusion from SBCA jurisdiction: Building plans approved by Cantonment Board engineering branch, which applicants often find faster

Common Disputes and Litigation

Despite cantonment jurisdiction, some Malir City Phase 1 plots are under litigation. Common causes:
1. Multiple allotments on same plot due to data entry error in 2016 ballot
2. Inheritance disputes (deceased applicant's legal heirs contesting)
3. Land acquisition disputes at the perimeter of Malir Cantonment where civilian land was incorporated

The Cantonment Tribunal (not SHC) has jurisdiction over most MCDA disputes. The process is faster than civil courts but outcomes can take 2–4 years to conclude.
